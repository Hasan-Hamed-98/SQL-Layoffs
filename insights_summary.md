# 📊 Insights Summary

## 📌 Overview

This analysis explores global layoff trends between 2020 and 2023, focusing on companies, industries, countries, and time-based patterns.

---

## 🏢 Key Insight 1: Tech Companies Lead Layoffs

Companies such as Amazon, Google, and Meta recorded the highest number of layoffs.

**Interpretation:**

* These companies experienced rapid growth during earlier periods
* Workforce reductions likely reflect market corrections and cost optimization

---

## 🏭 Key Insight 2: Most Affected Industries

The industries with the highest layoffs include:

* Consumer
* Retail
* Transportation

**Interpretation:**

* These sectors are sensitive to economic fluctuations
* Changes in consumer demand and supply chain disruptions contributed to workforce reductions

---

## 🌍 Key Insight 3: Geographic Concentration

The United States recorded the highest number of layoffs, followed by countries like India and the Netherlands.

**Interpretation:**

* The U.S. has a high concentration of large tech companies
* Global economic trends heavily influence layoffs in major economies

---

## 📈 Key Insight 4: Layoff Trends Over Time

* Layoffs increased significantly from late 2022 to early 2023
* Rolling totals show a sharp upward trend during this period

**Interpretation:**

* This spike may be linked to:

  * Post-pandemic market adjustments
  * Inflation and rising interest rates
  * Over-hiring during growth periods

---

## 🏆 Key Insight 5: Top Companies by Year

Each year had different companies leading layoffs:

* 2020: Booking.com
* 2021: Katerra
* 2022: Meta
* 2023: Google

**Interpretation:**

* Layoff patterns vary yearly depending on industry conditions
* Highlights shifting economic pressures across sectors

---

## 📊 Key Insight 6: Companies with 100% Layoffs

Some companies laid off their entire workforce (`percentage_laid_off = 1`).

**Interpretation:**

* Indicates company shutdowns or severe financial distress
* Represents extreme cases compared to gradual downsizing

---

## 🧠 Overall Conclusion

The data reveals that layoffs are influenced by:

* Macroeconomic conditions
* Industry-specific challenges
* Company growth cycles

The period between 2022 and 2023 stands out as the most significant phase of workforce reductions.

---

* Analyze layoffs by funding stage

* Build predictive models for future layoff trends
